## Audience-Specific Angles

**For young voters / students:**
> College students: your campus ID might not count as voter ID under new laws. Your out-of-state license might not work at local polls. Check your state's rules NOW — not the week before the election. Register where it makes sense for you, and request an absentee ballot early if voting from your home state.

**For parents / older adults:**
> Do your adult children have their birth certificates? Do they know where to find them? New voter ID laws could require proof of citizenship just to register. Help the people you love get ready before November.

**For people who changed their name:**
> Changed your name after marriage, divorce, or for any reason? Your birth certificate doesn't match your ID anymore — and that could block you from voting under new rules. Gather your marriage certificate, divorce decree, or court order now. Don't wait.

**For naturalized citizens:**
> If you lost your Naturalization Certificate, you need to start the replacement process immediately. USCIS Form N-565 takes 5–8 months to process. If you wait past April 2026, you will likely miss the November election. Fee waivers are available. VoteRiders can help for free.

**For people on a tight budget:**
> Worried about the cost of getting ID documents? Help exists. VoteRiders covers document costs for free. Many states waive birth certificate fees for people on public assistance. Dial 2-1-1 for local help. Your vote shouldn't depend on your bank account.

**For community organizers / nonprofits:**
> Share this free, nonpartisan voter readiness guide with your community. It covers new ID requirements, a step-by-step document timeline, costs and wait times, and links to free help. Print it, text it, post it. Help the people you serve show up ready in November.
